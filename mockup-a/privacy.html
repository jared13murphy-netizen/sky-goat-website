Privacy Policy

Effective June 8, 2026 · Last updated June 22, 2026

The short version. Sky Goat works fully anonymously — no account required. If you decide to sign in (with Apple, one tap, no passwords), we sync your flight history across devices and let you post in-flight photos privately to your own account. Either way, we don't sell your data and we don't email-spam you. We show ads via Google AdMob — opt in for relevant ads, opt out for generic ones; you see ads either way.

That's it. No newsletter signup. No abandoned-cart emails. No selling your data, because there's no incentive for us to do so.

What Sky Goat collects, in detail

Stored on your device only — never sent to us (anonymous mode)

  • Your flight history. When you're not signed in, routes you've watched, miles flown, and points of interest you've seen are stored only in iOS AsyncStorage. We don't have access to it. Deleting the app deletes it.
  • Your preferences. Category filters, settings choices. Stored locally.
  • Your boarding pass scan results. The PDF417 barcode is decoded entirely on-device by iOS's camera APIs; we never see the raw image. The decoded flight identifier is what's sent to FlightAware to look up the flight.

Sent to us — when you sign in or contact us

  • Email (when you sign in with Apple — this may be your real address or an Apple-generated @privaterelay.appleid.com alias; both work the same. Also if you write to hello@sky-goat.com or contact support).
  • Apple user ID (a stable opaque identifier Apple gives us so we can look up your account on subsequent sign-ins; not visible or usable outside Sky Goat).
  • First name (only if you allow Apple to share it on first sign-in; optional, used to personalize the app).
  • Flight history (once you sign in, the flights you save are synced to our servers so you can see them across devices).
  • Photos you post as Moments (only when you tap "Add a Moment" on a boarding-pass-verified flight; stored on Cloudflare R2 and viewable only by you in V1).
  • Reactions (the emoji you tap on your own Moments).
  • Coarse location (only when you tap "Find flights near me" — used once, not tracked in the background).

Third-party APIs we call on your behalf

When you open Sky Goat or start a flight, the app makes HTTPS requests to:

  • Apple (Sign in with Apple) — only when you tap the sign-in button. Apple authenticates you and hands us an ID token + email. You stay logged in with a Sky Goat JWT after that; we don't hit Apple again until you sign out and back in.
  • Sky Goat backend (hosted on Railway) — once signed in, the app calls our own backend to sync flights, upload Moments, and fetch reactions. Photos themselves live on Cloudflare R2.
  • FlightAware AeroAPI — flight position, filed routes, ETAs. They see the flight number you're tracking and your device's IP address.
  • ESPN public scoreboard — sports scores for "your team has a game tonight" cards. They see your device's IP.
  • NWS (National Weather Service) — weather at your destination. They see your device's IP.
  • ArcGIS World Imagery — satellite tiles for the postcard hero image. They see your device's IP.
  • Ground Goat API — active land listings near your flight path. They see your device's IP. Operated by Ground Goat, the same company that makes Sky Goat. See Ground Goat's privacy policy →

For the third-party content APIs (FlightAware, ESPN, NWS, ArcGIS, Ground Goat), we don't proxy these requests; your phone talks to them directly. We don't see what they return.

Google AdMob ads

Sky Goat displays occasional native ads in the Goat Feed via Google AdMob. AdMob shows ads regardless of your tracking choice, but it serves them differently based on what you choose:

  • You allow tracking (the iOS App Tracking Transparency prompt that pops up on first launch): AdMob uses your Apple IDFA to show more relevant ads. They may share data with advertisers.
  • You decline tracking: AdMob still shows ads, but they're non-personalized — basically anonymous. No IDFA shared.

You can change this any time in Settings → Sky Goat → Allow Tracking. Google's privacy policy → covers AdMob in detail.

What we don't do

  • We don't require an account. The whole app works anonymously.
  • We don't track your location in the background.
  • We don't sell, lease, or trade your information to anyone.
  • We don't send marketing emails. The email we have (if you signed in) is used for account recovery and important service notices only.
  • We don't use cookies. This is an iOS app, not a website.
  • We don't show your Moments to anyone else. In V1, Moments are strictly private — only visible to you, signed in to your own account.
  • We don't analyze the contents of your photos. They're stored as opaque blobs in R2; we never run image classification on them.

Children

Sky Goat is rated 4+ and is suitable for all ages. We don't knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children under 13. If you're a parent and believe your child has somehow sent us information (probably by writing to hello@sky-goat.com), email us and we'll delete it.

Your rights

If you've never signed in, there's nothing in our systems tied to you. Delete the app and that's the end of it.

If you have signed in, you can:

  • Delete your account any time from Settings → Delete my account inside the app. Your account is immediately deactivated — your session token is invalidated and the account is no longer accessible. Your flight history, Moments (and the underlying photos on Cloudflare R2), and reactions are scheduled for permanent removal. We perform hard-deletion on a periodic basis; email info@groundgoat.com from the address on the account if you want immediate hard-deletion.
  • Delete individual Moments from the moment card in the app.
  • Sign out from Settings → Sign out if you just want to stop syncing — your data stays safe and is restored when you sign back in.
  • Email us at hello@sky-goat.com to request an export of your data or to ask any question about it. We aim to reply within a few business days.

The rights granted by the California Consumer Privacy Act, GDPR, etc. (access, correction, deletion, portability) are honored — for most of them the in-app Delete button is the fastest path, but you can also reach out by email.

Changes to this policy

If we change anything material, we'll update the "Last updated" date at the top, post the change here, and (for changes that affect existing users meaningfully) surface it in the app the next time you open it.
